OPTrho

Computes rho function for optimal weight function

More About

Yohai and Zamar (1997) showed that the $\rho$ function given above is optimal in the following highly desirable sense: the final M estimate has a breakdown point of one-half and minimizes the maximum bias under contamination distributions (locally for small fraction of contamination), subject to achieving a desidered nominal asymptotic efficiency when the data are Gaussian. \[ \label{opt} \rho(x) = \begin{cases} 1.3846 \left( \frac{x}{c} \right)^2 \qquad |x| \leq \frac{2}{3} c \\ 0.5514-2.6917\left( \frac{x}{c} \right)^2+10.7668\left( \frac{x}{c} \right)^4-11.6640\left( \frac{x}{c} \right)^6+4.0375\left( \frac{x}{c} \right)^8 \qquad \frac{2}{3} c < |x| \leq c \\ 1 \qquad |x| >c \end{cases} \]
