ASpsider

Computes derivative of psi function (second derivative of rho function) for Andrew's sine function

More About

Function ASpsider transforms vector x as follows \[ ASpsi(u)= \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} (1/c) \cos(u/c) & |u/c| \leq \pi \\ 0 & |u/c|> \pi \\ \end{array} \right. \] Remark: Andrew's sine functionon is almost linear around $u = 0$ in accordance with Winsor's principle that all distributions are normal in the middle. This means that $\psi (u)/u$ is approximately constant over the linear region of $\psi$, so the points in that region tend to get equal weight.
