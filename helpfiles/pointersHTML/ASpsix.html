ASpsix

Computes psi function (derivative of rho function) times x for Andrew's sine function

More About

Function ASpsix transforms vector u as follows \[ ASpsix(u)= \left\{ \begin{array}{cc} (u/c) sin(u/c) & |u/c| \leq \pi \\ 0 & |u/c|> \pi \\ \end{array} \right. \] Remark: Andrew's psi-function is almost linear around u = 0 in accordance with Winsor's principle that all distributions are normal in the middle. This means that \psi (u)/u is approximately constant over the linear region of \psi, so the points in that region tend to get equal weight.
